01 / 23 Personal
Showa Era

To Gunma Prefecture

Letter
群馬縣太田市　大橋義雄 様

拝啓
御無沙汰致シテオリマスガ、皆様ニハ御變リナク御過ゴシノコトト存ジマス。

先日ハ大變御世話ニナリ有難ウ御座イマシタ。季節ノ變リ目デスノデ御身體ヲ御大事ニシテ下サイ。又近イウチニ御會イ出來ルコトヲ樂シミニシテオリマス。
敬具
To: Mr. Ohashi Yoshio, Ota City, Gunma

"Dear Sir,

I apologize for my silence, but I trust everyone is well.

Thank you for your hospitality the other day. As we are at the change of seasons, please take care. I look forward to seeing you again soon.
Respectfully yours"
Note: A warm letter to Gunma Prefecture expressing gratitude for hospitality. Ota City was known for its aircraft manufacturing industry during this era.

06 / 23 Farm News
Showa Era

News from the Farm

Letter
大橋 様

此方ノ様子ヲ御知ラセ致シマス。農作業モ順調ニ進ンデオリ、今年ノ収穫モ期待出來サウデス。田圃ノ稲モスクスクト育ッテオリマス。

又機會ガ御座イマシタラ御會イシタイト思ヒマス。
敬具
To: Mr. Ohashi

"I am writing to inform you of our situation. The farm work is progressing smoothly, and we can expect a good harvest this year. The rice in the paddies is growing vigorously.

If the opportunity arises, I would like to meet.
Respectfully yours"
Note: A rural correspondent sharing news about the farm. Rice farming was the backbone of rural Japan—harvest news carried both economic and emotional weight.
07 / 23 Military Mail
Wartime

Letter from Training

Full Message
謹啓
益々御清栄の段大慶に存じます。小生も此方にて元気に勤務しております。毎日訓練に励んでおりますが、皆様のご健康をお祈り申し上げます。内地の様子はいかがでしょうか。此方は寒さも厳しくなってまいりましたが、身体には気をつけて頑張っております。季節柄お身体ご自愛ください。
敬具
"Most Respectfully,

I am greatly pleased to hear of your continued prosperity. I too am serving here in good spirits, devoting myself to training every day. I pray for everyone's good health at home.

How are things in the homeland? The cold here has become severe, but I am taking care of my health and doing my best. Please take care of yourself.

Respectfully yours"
Note: The soldier uses reassuring language typical of military correspondence. The term "naichi" (homeland) was commonly used by soldiers abroad to refer to mainland Japan.
08 / 23 Autumn
Early Autumn

Saturday Evening Letter

Letter
土曜ノ夜

早々ト御返事頂キ誠ニ有難ウ御座イマス。貴方様カラノ御便リヲ讀ミ大變嬉シク思ヒマシタ。

御依頼ノ件、承知致シマシタ。近イウチニ必ズ御届ケ致シマス。田舎ハ大分涼シクナッテマイリマシタ。

敬具
"Saturday Evening

Thank you very much for your prompt reply. I was very happy to read your letter.

Regarding your request, I understand. I will certainly deliver it soon. The countryside has become quite cool.

Respectfully yours"
Note: Written on a Saturday evening as autumn arrives. The writer's obvious pleasure at receiving a letter and promise to fulfill a request shows the reciprocal nature of these relationships.
09 / 23 Summer
Midsummer

Summer Inquiry

Letter
拝啓
酷暑ノ候、皆様ニハ益々御健勝ノコトト御慶ビ申上候。

先日御話シ致候件、目下検討中ニ御座候。結果ガ出マシタラ直グニ御連絡致候。

暑サ厳シキ折、御身體御自愛下サレ度御願申上候。
敬具
"Dear Sir,

In this season of severe heat, I am pleased to hear of everyone's continued good health.

Regarding the matter we discussed, I am currently considering it. I will contact you immediately when I reach a conclusion.

In this severe heat, I pray you take care of yourself.
Respectfully yours"
Note: "Kokusho no kou" (season of severe heat) places this in peak summer—late July to mid-August. The seasonal reference expressed genuine concern for the recipient's wellbeing.
10 / 23 Personal
Showa Era

Apology for Silence

Letter
大橋 様

長ラク御無沙汰致シマシテ申譯御座イマセン。

當方モ一同無事ニ過ゴシテオリマス。最近ハ仕事モ忙シク、ナカナカ筆ヲ取ル暇ガ御座イマセンデシタ。御許シ下サイ。

次回御會イスル際ニハ、ユックリ御話シ出來レバト思ヒマス。
敬具
To: Mr. Ohashi

"I deeply apologize for my long silence.

All of us here are passing the days safely. Recently work has been busy, and I haven't had time to write. Please forgive me.

When we meet next, I hope we can talk at leisure.
Respectfully yours"
Note: A heartfelt apology for delayed correspondence. The guilt and hope for future conversation reveals the emotional weight these letters carried.
11 / 23 Formal
Showa Era

Request for a Visit

Letter
拝啓
過日ハ格別ノ御高配ヲ賜リ誠ニ有難ウ御座イマシタ。御蔭様デ無事ニ事ガ運ビマシタ。

改メテ御禮ヲ申上タク、近日中ニ御伺ヒ致シタイト存ジマスガ、御都合ノ宜シイ日時ヲ御知ラセ頂ケレバ幸甚ニ存ジマス。

敬具
"Dear Sir,

I sincerely thank you for your exceptional consideration the other day. Thanks to your kindness, the matter was concluded successfully.

I wish to express my gratitude properly in person and would like to visit in the near future. I would be grateful if you could inform me of a convenient time.

Respectfully yours"
Note: An exemplary formal thank-you letter. The writer wishes to express gratitude face-to-face—a gesture carrying greater social weight than written thanks alone.

14 / 23 Late Autumn
Late Autumn

Returning a Borrowed Book

Letter
拝啓
晩秋ノ候、イカガ御過ゴシデショウカ。

此度ハ、長ラク御借リシテオリマシタ本ヲ御返シ致シマス。大變勉強ニナリマシタ。有難ウ御座イマシタ。

季節柄、御風邪ナド召サレマセヌヨウ御自愛下サイ。
敬具
"Dear Sir,

How are you in this late autumn season?

I am now returning the book I borrowed for so long. It was very educational. Thank you very much.

Please take care not to catch cold.
Respectfully yours"
Note: Before public libraries were common, lending books among acquaintances was an important form of cultural exchange. The writer's gratitude shows the social bonds strengthened by such sharing.
15 / 23 Follow-up
Showa Era

Requesting a Response

Letter
前略
先日ノ件、御検討頂ケマシタデショウカ。御忙シイトコロ恐縮デスガ、御返事頂ケレバ幸イニ存ジマス。

詳シイ内容ニ付キマシテハ、改メテ御會イシタ際ニ御説明サセテ頂キマス。
草々
"Dispensing with formalities,

Have you been able to consider the matter? I am sorry to trouble you when busy, but I would appreciate a reply.

I will explain the details when we meet.
Hastily yours"
Note: The informal "zenryaku...soso" indicates either familiarity or urgency that overrides normal politeness. The writer persistently but diplomatically requests a response.
16 / 23 Reply
Showa Era

Agreement to Proposal

Letter
拝復
御手紙有難ウ御座イマシタ。

當方ト致シマシテハ、先方ノ御提案ニ同意致シテオリマス。具體的ナ日程等ヲ御相談サセテ頂ケレバト存ジマス。

取急ギ御返事マデ。
敬具
"In Reply,

Thank you for your letter.

On our part, we agree with the other party's proposal. I would like to discuss the specific schedule and details.

This is a hasty reply.
Respectfully yours"
Note: The opening "haifuku" marks this as a reply letter. The writer agrees to a proposal and wishes to arrange scheduling—perhaps for a meeting or transaction.

Showa Era

Awaiting Response

Letter
前略
御無沙汰致シテオリマス。

先般御願イ致シマシタ件ニ付キマシテ、御回答頂ケレバ幸甚ニ存ジマス。御不明ナ點ガ御座イマシタラ御氣輕ニ御問合セ下サイ。
草々
"Dispensing with formalities,

I apologize for my silence.

Hastily yours"
Note: Patient but persistent—the writer offers to clarify questions, a diplomatic way to encourage a reply without seeming demanding.
